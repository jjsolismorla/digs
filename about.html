About me

I was born in the coast of Ecuador in 1991, in a period of time that carried with it the aftermath of several political wrongdoings in South America, each of which deserve some revision.

My research and projects explore how different events in South American history interconnect. With over a decade of experience in digital communications, I try my best to harness the power of digital media to invite people affected by these events to co-create experiences with my work.

As an openly gay man, you will notice that a lot of my work is seen through the lens of sexuality and cultural paradigms around shame. This exploration, I hope, can help anyone interested in taking a closer look at how sexuality dialogues with other aspects of our lives, and how cultural paradigms that seek to obscure it or punish it can have detrimental effects in our well-being, some of it lasting for generations.
